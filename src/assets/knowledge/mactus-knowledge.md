# Compliance Products

## Overview
Mactus compliance products address documentation gaps in pharmaceutical manufacturing that create audit risk. Each product replaces manual paper-based processes with electronic, 21 CFR Part 11 compliant records that are tamper-proof and audit-ready.

**The five compliance products are:**
1. SACS — Smart Access Control System

3. ASDS — Automated Solution Dispensing System

## SACS (Smart Access Control System)
### Purpose
SACS is a paperless entry-exit management solution for cleanrooms and restricted pharmaceutical environments. It replaces manual logbooks with a biometrically authenticated, SOP-enforced, electronically recorded system. Every entry is timestamped and signed. No manual reconciliation required.

### Confirmed Features
- **Biometric Authentication:** Fingerprint and face recognition for every entry.
- **SOP-Enforced Gowning Sequence:** System enforces the full gowning procedure before granting entry. Hard interlocking prevents door opening unless all conditions are met.
- **Training Verification:** Syncs with HRMS to allow only certified, currently trained staff into specific areas.
- **Tailgate Detection:** High-precision overhead IR or AI-based vision sensors count individuals and alarm if multiple people enter on a single badge-in.
- **21 CFR Part 11 Compliance:** Full electronic records, electronic signatures, tamper-proof audit trail.
- **Real-Time Dashboard:** Live monitoring of every entry, exit, and violation across the facility.
- **Audit Readiness:** Instant export of signed audit trails for USFDA, MHRA, EU GMP, WHO-GMP inspections.
- **Deployment:** Typically 6–8 weeks end-to-end.

### Frequently Asked Questions
**Q: What is SACS?**
A: SACS replaces manual entry-exit logbooks in cleanrooms with a paperless, 21 CFR Part 11 compliant system that biometrically authenticates personnel, enforces SOPs, and maintains an electronic audit trail.

**Q: Does SACS prevent tailgating?**
A: Yes, using high-precision overhead IR sensors or AI-based vision sensors to count individuals and trigger an alarm if the count exceeds authorized badge-ins.

**Q: Which regulatory standards does SACS meet?**
A: 21 CFR Part 11 and EU GMP Annex 1.

## IRS (Intervention Recording System)
### Purpose
IRS automatically records every aseptic intervention in real-time during pharmaceutical fill-finish operations. It digitizes open door and glove-port interventions captured by sensors — without requiring operator input — and associates each intervention to the correct operator via access control integration. Aligned with EU GMP Annex 1 (2022).

### Confirmed Features
- **Auto Sensor Capture:** Non-contact magnetic sensors and IR curtains detect port opening and hand entry automatically.
- **Operator Binding:** Syncs with access control to automatically attribute interventions to the specific operator at the line.
- **SOP Guidance:** Displays step-by-step recovery procedures on-screen during an intervention.
- **Batch Control:** Enforces maximum allowable interventions per batch; triggers supervisor alerts when thresholds are approached.
- **Reporting & Trending:** Automatic generation of PQR/APR-ready data with trend analysis.
- **APS / Media Fill Support:** Specific mode for Aseptic Process Simulation to track complex intervention sequences.
- **21 CFR Part 11 Compliance:** Electronic records and signatures for every intervention event.
- **Deployment:** Typically 6–8 weeks.

### Frequently Asked Questions
**Q: What is IRS?**
A: IRS automatically records aseptic interventions in fill-finish lines using door/port sensors. Every intervention is captured without operator self-reporting, attributed to the correct operator, and stored in a 21 CFR Part 11 compliant audit trail.

**Q: How is it different from manual recording?**
A: Manual recording relies on operators to self-report their interventions, which creates data integrity gaps. IRS uses sensors to auto-detect and record every intervention the moment it happens.

## ASDS (Automated Solution Dispensing System)
### Purpose
ASDS is a mobile, stainless-steel (SS) fabricated automated cleaning solution preparation unit for pharmaceutical and healthcare facilities. It mixes precise dilutions of disinfectants, detergents, and IPA with purified water on demand — eliminating manual mixing errors and providing a complete 21 CFR Part 11 compliant audit trail for every dispense.

### Confirmed Features
- **Solvents-Driven Preparation:** Up to 6 configurable solvents (disinfectant, IPA, detergent, sporicide). Rolvents are locked under change control.
- **Volumetric Accuracy:** Exact stock and purified water ratios dispensed — no "close enough" tolerance.
- **On-Demand, No Waste:** Solutions prepared only when needed, eliminating over-preparation and waste of expensive concentrates.
- **21 CFR Part 11 Compliance:** User management, electronic signatures, automated record-keeping for every dispense (solvent, volume, operator, time).
- **Mobile Design:** SS-fabricated with castors — moves between cleanrooms as needed.
- **Deployment:** Typically 6–8 weeks.

### Frequently Asked Questions
**Q: What is ASDS?**
A: ASDS is an automated system that prepares cleaning and disinfection solutions to precise solvent-controlled ratios, eliminating manual mixing errors and providing a full electronic record of every dispensed solution.

**Q: How many solvents can ASDS handle?**
A: Up to 6 different disinfectant/cleaning solution solvents per unit, all locked under change control.

## MPATS — Mactus Environmental Monitoring (Media Plate Tracking System)
### Purpose
MPATS digitises the complete lifecycle of environmental monitoring media plates used in pharmaceutical cleanrooms. Each plate receives a QR code at inventory and is scanned at every stage (exposure, incubation, reading, destruction), ensuring full traceability, automated reconciliation, and 21 CFR Part 11 compliance. Prevents unaccounted plates at batch closure.

### Confirmed Features
- **QR-Code Lifecycle Tracking:** Tracks settle plates, active air, surface, and personnel monitoring plates from inventory to destruction.
- **Automatic Reconciliation:** Real-time count of plates issued, used, and discarded. Missing-plate alerts at every stage transition.
- **Electronic CFU Readings:** CFU counts entered via guided HMI, electronically signed, and auto-compared against alert/action limits.
- **Compliance:** EU GMP Annex 1 and 21 CFR Part 11 aligned electronic records.
- **Integration:** Connects to incubator monitoring, EMS, LIMS, MES, and BMS.
- **Deployment:** Typically 10–14 weeks.

## IVBLT (Intravenous Bag Leak Tester)
### Purpose
IVBLT applies constant, controlled pressure to IV bags to detect sealing defects, pinholes, and port leaks invisible to the naked eye. It is built for 100% in-line testing on large volume parenteral (LVP) manufacturing lines. Non-destructive — passing bags proceed directly to the next step.

### Confirmed Features
- **High-Sensitivity Leak Detection:** Detects pinholes and sealing defects below 0.2mm — invisible to manual visual inspection.
- **Non-Destructive:** Passing bags proceed to labelling/packaging without modification.
- **Size-Adjusted Pressure:** Automatically adjusts test pressure per bag size (50 mL, 100 mL, 250 mL, 500 mL, 1000 mL).
- **Bag Status Reconciliation:** Real-time tracking of tested, passed, failed, and quarantined bag counts per batch.
- **21 CFR Part 11 Compliance:** Electronic records for every test event — bag ID, pressure, result, operator, timestamp.
- **Regulatory Compliance:** cGMP and GDP compliant; audit-ready for USFDA, EU GMP, MHRA, WHO-GMP.
- **Deployment:** Typically 6–8 weeks.

### Frequently Asked Questions
**Q: Does IVBLT destroy the bags it tests?**
A: No. IVBLT is non-destructive. Bags that pass the pressure test proceed directly to the next production step.

**Q: Which bag sizes are supported?**
A: 50 mL, 100 mL, 250 mL, 500 mL, and 1000 mL. Test pressure is automatically adjusted per size.

## EMS (Environmental Monitoring System)
### Purpose
Mactus EMS continuously monitors temperature, relative humidity, and differential pressure across pharmaceutical cleanrooms, storage rooms, and production zones. Data is logged immutably with alarms and audit-ready reports.

The relationship between BMS and EMS: **"BMS keeps the environment in control. EMS proves it stayed in control."**

### Confirmed Features
- **Continuous Monitoring:** Real-time temperature, RH, and differential pressure sampling at configured intervals.
- **Local Alarming:** Visual and audible alarms at room level on local room displays.
- **Centralized Alarm Management:** All alarms consolidated on a central dashboard.
- **Immutable Data Storage:** ALCOA+ aligned, tamper-proof database. Data cannot be altered after capture.
- **21 CFR Part 11 Compliance:** Electronic signatures for alarm acknowledgments, role-based access, full audit trail.
- **GMP-Approved Devices:** Qualified sensors with calibration records maintained per installation.
- **Custom Reporting:** Reports configurable by room, area, date range, parameter, or batch — formatted for APR/PQR.
- **Remote Access:** Authorized users can monitor parameters and acknowledge alarms remotely.
- **Network Resilience:** Local data buffering at sensor level; synchronizes to server on reconnection — no data gaps.
- **Integration:** Often delivered as an integrated pair with BMS. Also integrates with LIMS, MES, and electronic batch records.
- **Deployment:** Typically 10–16 weeks for a single facility.

### Frequently Asked Questions
**Q: What parameters does EMS monitor?**
A: Temperature, relative humidity (RH), and differential pressure as standard. Additional parameters (particle counts, CO2, airflow velocity) can be integrated.

**Q: What is an alert limit vs. action limit?**
A: An alert limit is an early warning threshold. An action limit is a breach of the validated range requiring immediate investigation. Both trigger alarms and are logged to the audit trail.

**Q: Is EMS data 21 CFR Part 11 compliant?**
A: Yes. All environmental data is stored in a tamper-proof, immutable database with electronic signatures and role-based access.

# IIoT Implementations (Industrial Internet of Things)

## Overview
Mactus IIoT implementations layer edge gateways, secure data pipelines, and analytics over the BMS, EMS, and process equipment that pharmaceutical and industrial facilities already run — turning existing operational data into a real-time view of equipment health, utility consumption, and production performance. Validated systems are never disturbed.

**Core positioning:** "The data layer over the systems you already run."

## What IIoT Is (vs. BMS/SCADA)
- BMS and SCADA control equipment within their own scope.
- IIoT reads from BMS, SCADA, PLCs, smart meters, and sensors — brings data into one unified analytics platform.
- BMS tells you the chiller is running. IIoT tells you the chiller is running 12% less efficiently and needs maintenance in 30 days.

## Five Architecture Components
1. **Sensor Layer** — Smart sensors for vibration, current, temperature on equipment not yet instrumented. Existing PLC data read directly.
2. **Edge Gateways** — Industrial-grade devices aggregating data, translating protocols (OPC-UA, Modbus, BACnet, MQTT, REST), buffering through outages, isolating OT from IT networks.
3. **Secure Data Pipeline** — TLS encryption, segmented network architecture, certificate-based device identity, IEC 62443 compliant design.
4. **Analytics & Visualisation Platform** — Real-time dashboards for engineering, maintenance, and leadership; trending, alerting, historical analysis, export capabilities.
5. **Integration with Systems Above** — Data pushed to MES, EBR, LIMS, ERP, CMMS where it adds value.

## Key Use Cases
- **Energy & Utility Analytics** — 8–18% energy cost reduction in year one (typical, varies by baseline).
- **Equipment Health & Maintenance** — Condition-based maintenance from vibration/current/temperature analysis.
- **Production Performance & OEE** — Line-level availability, performance, quality losses; Pareto downtime analysis.
- **Utility & Infrastructure Monitoring** — Centralized view of chillers, compressed air, PW, WFI, clean steam, process gases.
- **Sustainability & ESG Reporting** — Automated Scope 1 and 2 emissions tracking.
- **Cold-Chain & Validated Storage** — Continuous monitoring of 2–8°C, -20°C, -80°C chambers.

## Four GxP Design Principles
1. **Don't Disturb What's Validated** — IIoT reads; does not write to validated systems unless explicitly scoped.
2. **Keep OT and IT Separated** — Edge gateways enforce the boundary per IEC 62443.
3. **Respect GxP Data Classification** — GxP data stays in validated source systems as the authoritative record.
4. **Build for the Team That Will Run It** — Handed over so the customer's team can operate independently.

## OT Cybersecurity
Every IIoT project includes:
- Network segmentation between OT and IT (IEC 62443 zone-and-conduit model)
- Certificate-based device identity and authentication
- TLS encryption in transit, AES at rest
- Role-based access control with multi-factor authentication
- Edge gateway hardening (minimal services, patching, signed firmware)
- Audit logging of every administrative action
- Incident response playbook handed to customer's IT/security teams

## Deployment Timeline
- Discovery & assessment phase: 2–3 weeks on-site.
- First focused phase (single plant, 1–2 use cases): 12–20 weeks end-to-end.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**Q: Will IIoT affect our validated systems?**
A: No. Mactus IIoT is read-only against validated systems by default. Validated systems continue to operate as before.

**Q: Cloud or on-premise?**
A: Either, or hybrid. Many pharma customers prefer on-premise with optional secure cloud connectivity for multi-site analytics.

**Q: Do we need new sensors?**
A: Most existing PLCs, BMS controllers, EMS sensors, smart meters can be read directly into IIoT. New sensors are added only where data gaps exist.
